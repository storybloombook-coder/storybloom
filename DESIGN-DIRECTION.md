# Design Direction — Storybloom

The look and feel this app is aiming for. This guides the visual restyle
(milestone 11) and the general "vibe" of every screen. It is NOT a v1 blocker —
build functional-first — but when it's time to make it beautiful, aim HERE, and
don't drift toward generic trends.

## One line
A cozy, hand-crafted storybook world with the tactile satisfaction of a
well-made old-school RPG menu — but calm, warm, and slow, because it's bedtime.

## The guiding tension (this is the whole identity)
TACTILE but CALM. The interface should feel physical and satisfying to touch —
but the energy is soft and unhurried, not snappy or loud. The reference feeling
is turning the thick page of a good board book, or a cozy RPG menu at low volume
— NOT a fast game UI with punchy "blip" sounds. Every satisfying detail is dialed
warm and gentle.

## Flavor: cozy / whimsical (NOT slick, NOT trendy)
- Warm, storybook, hand-crafted. Feels made by a person, not a template.
- Deliberately NOT following current app-design trends (no flat minimalism for
  its own sake, no generic SaaS look, no sterile white space). Character over
  fashion.
- Draws from the SPIRIT of old-school RPG menus: framed panels, a sense of
  craft, considered typography, a world you're stepping into — reinterpreted as
  cozy and child-warm rather than fantasy-epic.

## What makes it feel tactile & satisfying (all four matter)
1. Chunky bordered panels/frames around things — cards and menus have real
   visible frames with weight, like inventory slots or a storybook border.
   Soft, rounded, warm — not sharp metal or harsh pixel edges.
2. Satisfying button presses — buttons have depth and respond to touch: a gentle
   press-in, a soft settle. Physical, not flat. But SOFT (a cushioned press, not
   a hard click).
3. Smooth transitions between screens — pages and menus move with gentle,
   deliberate motion (ease-out, unhurried). Nothing jarring or fast. Think a
   page turning, a panel sliding warmly into place.
4. Sound + haptic feedback on taps — small, warm confirmations. A soft wooden
   tap, a gentle haptic tick. Quiet and cozy, NEVER a loud game chime. Should be
   easy to mute (bedtime = sometimes silent).

## Calm, because it's bedtime
- Warm, low-key palette (think lamplight, dusk, soft paper — not bright primary
  colors or high-contrast neon).
- Motion is slow and soothing. Animations ease gently; nothing snaps or bounces
  aggressively.
- The reading screen especially should feel like winding down: the page
  dominates, controls recede, sounds are the star.
- Any feedback (haptics/sound) is a soft touch, tuned so it never jolts a
  drowsy child or parent.

## Concretely, when building the restyle
- Typography: warm, rounded, characterful — a storybook feel, still very legible.
  Avoid cold system defaults for headings.
- Frames/panels: soft chunky borders, gentle rounded corners, subtle paper/warm
  texture is OK (used tastefully, not busy).
- Color: warm neutrals + lamplight tones; a small cozy accent palette. Dark/dusk
  variants suit the bedtime reading screen.
- Motion: ease-out, ~250-400ms, gentle. Page turns and panel slides, not fades
  that feel abrupt.
- Buttons: visible depth, press-in animation, spring back softly.
- Feedback: optional soft haptic + a quiet warm tap sound; global mute toggle.
- RESIST the urge to make it look like every other 2026 app. The whole point is
  that it feels hand-made and cozy, like a bedtime object, not a product.

## Guardrails
- This is the RESTYLE target (milestone 11), not a reason to over-invest in
  visuals early. Functional screens first; this vision comes last.
- Keep it accessible: warm and cozy must not mean low-contrast/unreadable.
  Legibility wins over mood where they conflict.
- Tactile feedback must be MUTABLE — a bedtime app sometimes needs to be silent.
- Don't let "RPG menu" pull it toward busy/ornate/fantasy clutter. Cozy and
  uncluttered. Warmth through craft, not decoration overload.

## Reference feeling (for the moodboard you'll gather)
When collecting design-reference screenshots, look for: cozy game menus (calm,
warm, framed), quality children's board-book design, warm storybook
illustration, soft "handmade" digital UI. AVOID: sleek fintech/SaaS UIs, cold
minimalism, bright cartoonish kid-app clutter, aggressive gamified interfaces.
